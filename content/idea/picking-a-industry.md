Assuming you don't currently have an idea or a set of ideas, how do you know how to search for good ideas? What defines a good idea? This section focuses on narrowing your focus to one specific industry, and exploring the space without commiting.

Before you brainstorm about what you should build, understand yourself and your limitations. What field/speciality do you have significant knowledge of? What do you do not know how to do yet? Can you code, can you manage money, are 

- If you're in highschool, pick something you're interested in or have some experience in. If you're planning to do Computer Science in college, consider that industry.

- If you're in the workforce, what industry are you in? What knowledge/connections of that industry do you have that puts you uniquly ahead

- If you're in college, consider your current major. If you're Aerospace Engineering like I was, pursue that industry.

Regardless of which industry make sure you have one of these traits before pursuing:

1. Money
2. Fame
3. Operational knowledge (ex: coding, engineering, maths)
4. Motivation to solve problems
5. Social skills 

If you have none of these, consider working on that before you continue.
