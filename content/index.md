So you want to be an entrepenuer?

![Startup journey](./assets/leonardo-dicaprio-money.gif)
About 80% of all startups fail over a 20 year period, with the highest failure rates in the first few years. About 10% of founders in their first year will fail, 20% second year, 35% in the third, and so on.

link: https://www.bls.gov/bdm/entrepreneurship/bdm_chart3.htm

Typically most struggle with one of many reasons, sometimes a combination:
-No market need (42%)
-Running out of cash (29%)
-Lacking the right team (23%)
-Weak business model (17%)
-Inadequate Marketing (14%)

In this guide I've synthesized some of the best information I've gathered from YCombinator, Alex Hormozi, Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston, and so many others. Occassionally I'll give links to those works because they're extensive and valuable in their own right. 

This is a growing page, so I'll occasionally add more information as I learn more about how founders are struggling.
