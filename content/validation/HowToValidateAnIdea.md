title: How to validate an Idea
---

Many founders validate ideas in completely the wrong way. BEFORE you write one line of code you need at least 20 customers saying they're having X problem. The way to go about this step is customer interviews. 

Before you send one message to anyone, you need to make an hypothesis about your idea. 

For instance, for us our hypothesis is/was: founders would want to improve their selling capability on demo calls.

That's it, it's that simple. All you want to do is state what your product aims to do. Here's a few other examples to get you started:

With the hypothesis made, now you need to brainstorm who would actually care about it. For Marsell, the answer is obviously founders. Others will have a harder time figuring out who to speak to but generally they need to be one of these three:

1. Decision Maker
2. Has Money
3. Cares about the problem

Now that you have an hypothesis and target demographic you will need to conduct interviews in way that doesn't directly point to your solution. Meaning, you want to validate the PROBLEM, not the solution. In fact, you should never mention the solution, expect maybe at the end if they're curious. You do not want lead with your plan at all to not bias their answers. Even if the data points against your understanding.

Your goal here is to learn what problems potential customers have and their motivations around them.
